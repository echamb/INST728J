welcome to my cooking page!

join me in my adventure to explore cultures around the world through traditions and innovations in cooking.

growing up, my fondest memories are of being in the kitchen cooking family meals with my dad. being one of four children, my parents would often have to come up with creative cooking solutions to feed us all food that we liked that was cheap and, ideally, fast. my dad would pull out frozen chicken or beef before leaving for work in the morning to let it defrost. after the long work day was over, he'd come home, change into his sweats, and get to cooking. the kitchen always smelled of rich spices stewing in the oven, roasting meats exploding with flavor, and sweet, sweaty vegetables caramelizing with butter and oil in a frying pan. my dad was, and still is, his most joyful and creative self when he's in the kitchen. i hope to bring the same joy and wonder to my own food-adventures explored here!

let's get started!

in honor of my sweet dad, i want to start this food adventure where it all began, right inside of our own kitchen. dad always said that you could tell the true quality of any restuarant by how they cooked one of the world's most humble dishes: roasted chicken.

Photo Credit: Jess Larson

here's what we'll need:

  • 1 roasting chicken
  • kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • bunch fresh thyme, plus 20 sprigs
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 head garlic, cut in half crosswise
  • 1/4 stick butter, melted
  • 1 yellow onion, thickly sliced
  • 4 carrots cut into 2-inch chunks
  • olive oil

cooking instructions

preheat oven to 425 degrees f.

  1. remove the chicken giblets. rinse the chicken inside and out. remove any excess fat and leftover pin feathers and pat the outside dry.
  2. liberally salt and pepper the inside of the chicken. stuff the cavity with the bunch of thyme, both halves of lemon, and all the garlic.
  3. brush the outside of the chicken with the butter and sprinkle again with salt and pepper.
  4. tie the legs together with kitchen string and tuck the wing tips under the body of the chicken.
  5. place the onions and carrots in a roasting pan. toss with salt, pepper, 20 sprigs of thyme, and olive oil. spread around the bottom of the roasting pan and place the chicken on top.
  6. roast the chicken for 1 1/2 hours, or until the juices run clear.
  7. remove the chicken and vegetables to a platter and cover with aluminum foil for about 20 minutes.
  8. slice the chicken onto a platter and serve it with the vegetables.

and there you have it! enjoy this simple dish with friends and family, just like dad would want :)
